# Comprehensive B2B Persona Library for Value Engineering

## Personas as Value Interpretation Lenses

This library defines **28 B2B personas** across **7 core industries** as interpretive frameworks that translate the same underlying capabilities into distinct value narratives. Each persona represents a specific economic worldview—what they prioritize, how they measure success, and what proof they require.

---

## Core Archetype Framework

### Archetype 1: The Economic Buyer (CFO/Finance)
**Value Orientation**: Cost reduction → Risk mitigation → Capital efficiency
**Core Question**: "What is the verified ROI and payback period?"

### Archetype 2: The Executive Sponsor (CEO/BU Head)
**Value Orientation**: Revenue uplift → Strategic agility → Competitive positioning
**Core Question**: "How does this accelerate our strategic priorities?"

### Archetype 3: The Operational Leader (VP Operations)
**Value Orientation**: Efficiency → Throughput → Quality → Scalability
**Core Question**: "Will this actually work in our operational reality?"

### Archetype 4: The Technical Evaluator (CIO/CTO/IT)
**Value Orientation**: Integration → Security → Scalability → Maintainability
**Core Question**: "Can we implement, integrate, and support this?"

### Archetype 5: The Growth Leader (CRO/VP Sales/Marketing)
**Value Orientation**: Revenue uplift → Customer acquisition → Market expansion
**Core Question**: "How fast can we see pipeline and revenue impact?"

### Archetype 6: The Risk Guardian (CRO/Compliance/Security)
**Value Orientation**: Risk mitigation → Regulatory compliance → Audit readiness
**Core Question**: "What could go wrong and how do we prevent it?"

### Archetype 7: The Operational User (End User/Manager)
**Value Orientation**: Usability → Time savings → Error reduction → Job satisfaction
**Core Question**: "Will this make my job easier or harder?"

## Industry 1: Manufacturing

### MFG-001: Plant Manager (Operational Leader)
**Archetype**: Operational Leader
**Seniority**: Director
**Decision Authority**: FINAL (for operational spend)

**Context**:
- Company Maturity: Enterprise/Regulated
- Company Size: $500M-$5B revenue, 1,000-10,000 employees
- Market Context: High-volume discrete or process manufacturing

**Key Objectives**:
- Meet daily/weekly production targets (OEE >85%)
- Control variable manufacturing costs
- Maintain workforce safety (zero incidents)
- Reduce unplanned downtime

**KPIs**:
- Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE)
- Cost per unit produced
- Production volume vs. plan
- Safety incident rate
- Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF)

**Pain Points**:
- Unplanned equipment downtime disrupting schedules
- Rising labor and energy costs compressing margins
- Quality escapes causing customer complaints
- Maintenance backlogs and spare parts shortages
- Difficulty retaining skilled operators

**Decision Criteria**:
1. Proven impact on OEE in similar facilities
2. Implementation timeline without production disruption
3. Integration with existing MES/ERP/SCADA
4. Total cost of ownership (3-5 year view)
5. Vendor's manufacturing domain expertise

**Typical Objections**:
- "We can't stop production to implement this"
- "Our processes are too complex/custom"
- "Maintenance team is already overloaded"
- "We tried something similar before and it failed"

**Value Drivers**:
- **Revenue Uplift**: Increased throughput, reduced lost sales from stockouts
- **Cost Savings**: Energy efficiency, reduced scrap/rework, labor optimization
- **Risk Reduction**: Predictive maintenance preventing catastrophic failures

**Preferred Proof Points**:
- Case studies from named manufacturing customers
- Pilot program data from similar facilities
- Before/after OEE metrics
- Third-party analyst reports (Gartner, ARC Advisory)
- ROI calculator with their actual data
