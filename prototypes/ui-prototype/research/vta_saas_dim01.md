### Finding 6.2: SODA (Strategic Options Development and Analysis) Method

**Claim:** SODA uses interview and cognitive mapping to capture individual views of complex issues, then aggregates individual maps into group maps to facilitate negotiation about value systems, strategic issues, and option portfolios, with attention to political and process dynamics[^72^].

**Source:** University of Cambridge, Institute for Manufacturing
**URL:** https://www.ifm.eng.cam.ac.uk/research/dstools/soda/
**Date:** Unknown Date
**Excerpt:** "SODA uses interview and cognitive mapping to capture individual views of an issue. Group maps constructed through the aggregation of individual cognitive maps are used to facilitate negotiation about value/goal systems, key strategic issues, and option portfolios. As well as problem content, attention is paid to the affective, political, and process dynamics in the group."
**Context:** SODA was originally developed by Colin Eden at the University of Bath. The process uses two PCs, special software (COPE/Decision Explorer), large monitors, and is managed by two facilitators. It aims at groups of 4-10 participants and generates rich models rather than simplified abstractions.
**Confidence:** High

## 9. Summary: VTA Step 1 to SaaS Workflow Mapping

### How Digital Decision Context Framing Translates Manual VTA Step 1 into SaaS Capability

The academic Value Tree Analysis (VTA) defines "Decision Context" as "the setting in which the decision occurs, framed by administrative, political and social structures." In the manual process, defining this context requires answering five questions: What is important and relevant? What are the objectives? What is the real problem? Who are the parties involved? What information is available?

Our research reveals how modern SaaS platforms translate each of these five manual VTA questions into digital workflow capabilities:

#### 9.1 "What is important and relevant?" --> Digital Decision Framing Canvases

| Manual VTA Activity | SaaS Translation | Key Platforms |
|--------------------|------------------|---------------|
| Brainstorming what's relevant | Problem Framing Canvas with structured zones (WHAT, WHY, WHO, HOW) | Miro[^44^], Creately[^58^], Notion[^113^] |
| Assessing relevance | Evidence Gallery sections for data, quotes, screenshots | Miro[^41^], Lucidspark[^73^] |
| Determining importance | Weighted criteria with impact metrics and scoring | Miro[^51^], Notion[^123^], DPL[^100^] |

**Key Insight:** Digital canvases enforce structure through template-based "frames" that require users to document importance before proceeding. The Miro Problem Framing Canvas's five core elements (User Persona, Current/Desired State, Evidence Gallery, Impact Metrics, Problem Statement) directly map to the VTA question of what's important and relevant.

#### 9.2 "What are the objectives?" --> Guided Objective-Setting Workflows

| Manual VTA Activity | SaaS Translation | Key Platforms |
|--------------------|------------------|---------------|
| Defining objectives | Decision Templates with built-in objective fields | Cloverpop[^81^], Pyramid[^78^] |
| Aligning on goals | AI-assisted goal extraction from unstructured input | Cloverpop AI[^83^], Asana 5 Whys[^90^] |
| Setting measurables | OKR linking, KPI dashboards, impact tracking | Key2Success[^82^], Cloverpop Decision Bank[^79^] |

**Key Insight:** Decision Intelligence Platforms like Cloverpop explicitly embed objective-setting into Decision Playbooks, accelerating the process from 28 days to 7 days. The "Decision Board" concept in decision journals provides a space to "decompress thoughts without taking action" before formalizing objectives.

#### 9.3 "What is the real problem?" --> AI-Assisted Problem Definition

| Manual VTA Activity | SaaS Translation | Key Platforms |
|--------------------|------------------|---------------|
| Root cause analysis | 5 Whys digital templates with assigned owners | Asana[^90^], Miro[^41^] |
| Problem reframing | Abstraction Ladder, "How Might We" pivot tools | Miro[^41^], Design Sprint Academy[^45^] |
| Evidence gathering | AI-powered issue detection and sentiment analysis | Simply Stakeholders[^62^], Cloverpop D-Sight[^77^] |
| Problem structuring | Cognitive mapping, dialogue mapping, argument trees | Decision Explorer[^68^], Compendium[^52^], Kialo[^101^] |

**Key Insight:** This is the most mature area of digital translation. AI problem framing canvases (Miro) and decision mining from meeting transcripts (Cloverpop) represent significant advances over manual problem definition. SODA/Decision Explorer provides a 30+ year foundation of digital problem structuring that modern SaaS builds upon.

#### 9.4 "Who are the parties involved?" --> Digital Stakeholder Mapping & RACI

| Manual VTA Activity | SaaS Translation | Key Platforms |
|--------------------|------------------|---------------|
| Identifying stakeholders | Automatic stakeholder detection from notes/emails | Simply Stakeholders[^62^] |
| Mapping influence | Power-Interest Matrix with drag-and-drop visualization | Miro, Smaply, StakeWare[^27^] |
| Defining roles | RACI diagram templates with real-time collaboration | Miro[^36^], Asana, Monday.com |
| Tracking engagement | AI sentiment analysis and relationship network maps | Simply Stakeholders[^61^] |
| Decision rights | RAPID/RACI/DARE/PACE framework integration | Cloverpop[^88^] |

**Key Insight:** Modern stakeholder tools have evolved far beyond static org charts. Simply Stakeholders' AI-powered sentiment analysis (since 2017) and automatic relationship mapping represent a qualitative leap from manual stakeholder identification. Cloverpop's integration of four decision-rights frameworks (RAPID, RACI, DARE, PACE) directly addresses the political and social structures that frame decisions.

#### 9.5 "What information is available?" --> Data Integration & Decision Intelligence

| Manual VTA Activity | SaaS Translation | Key Platforms |
|--------------------|------------------|---------------|
| Gathering information | Universal data connectors to ERP, CRM, data warehouses | Pyramid[^78^], Aera[^111^], Cloverpop[^77^] |
| Assessing data quality | AI-powered data profiling and quality scoring | Aera Decision Data Model[^111^] |
| Information synthesis | NLP/GenAI summarization across data sources | Cloverpop D-Sight[^83^], Lucidspark AI[^73^] |

**Key Insight:** Gartner's Decision Intelligence Platform category (2024) formalizes the integration of data, analytics, and AI for decision support. The distinction between "DI vendors" and "DIP vendors" hinges on mandatory capabilities including decision modeling, composability, and decision monitoring -- all directly relevant to the VTA information-gathering step.

### Synthesis: The Digital Decision Context Framing Stack

The research reveals a five-layer capability stack for digital decision context framing in SaaS:

```
L5: DECISION INTELLIGENCE (Cloverpop, Pyramid, Aera)
     --> AI-driven synthesis, modeling, orchestration, learning
     
L4: STAKEHOLDER & RIGHTS MAPPING (Simply Stakeholders, Miro RACI)
     --> AI sentiment analysis, relationship networks, decision frameworks
     
L3: PROBLEM STRUCTURING (Decision Explorer, Kialo, Miro Canvases)
     --> Cognitive mapping, argument trees, structured canvases
     
L2: DECISION CANVAS (Miro, Mural, Lucidspark, Notion)
     --> Visual collaboration, templates, real-time brainstorming
     
L1: GUIDED ONBOARDING (Wizard UI, Checklists, Persona Segmentation)
     --> Contextual setup, progressive disclosure, personalization
```

### Key Gaps and Opportunities for VTA SaaS Translation

1. **Explicit Decision Context "Wizard":** No platform found offers a single guided wizard that specifically replicates the full VTA Step 1 sequence. The closest are Cloverpop's Decision Playbooks and Miro's Problem Framing templates, but these are fragmented across tools.

2. **Value Tree-Specific Onboarding:** Current onboarding patterns (tours, checklists, simulations) are generic. A VTA-specific onboarding flow would guide users through context definition with built-in value hierarchy suggestions.

3. **Integrated Political/Social Structure Mapping:** While stakeholder tools map influence, none explicitly address the "administrative, political and social structures" framing from the original VTA definition. This represents a design opportunity.

4. **Decision Context Persistence:** Most tools treat context framing as a one-time activity. Cloverpop's Decision Bank is the exception, creating institutional memory. VTA SaaS should maintain evolving decision context records.

5. **From Framing to Value Hierarchy:** The critical gap in the current market is the direct translation from decision context (Step 1) to value tree construction (Step 2). No platform found offers a seamless "context-to-tree" workflow.
